  2. A group of five people (Alejandro, Ben, Camille, Dalia, and Elias) all want to join three clubs (Xylophone, Yodeling, and Zoology).
    1. Define and draw the complete bipartite graph representing club memberships. How many memberships result?
    2. How many memberships would result for \(n\) people joining \(m\) clubs?
  3. Fred, along with the five people from the previous problem need to split into two groups of three for a project. However, there is drama! Alejandro refuses to work with Dalia; Ben had a fight with Elias; Camille "just can't even" with either Alejandro or Ben.
    1. Define and draw a graph \(W\), with six vertices, to represent who can work with whom.
    2. Find \(\bar{W}\), the complement of \(W\).
    3. Show that it is possible to create two groups of three by partitioning \(\bar{W}\) into a bipartite graph with three vertices on each side.

  9. A school district is planning the location of a single bus stop. In the graph below, vertices represent neighborhoods with students. The large vertex labeled \(S\) is the school. Edges represent routes between neighborhoods. The weight on each route is the time it takes in minutes to walk that route.
    If there were no bus stop at all, then the average travel time (per neighborhood) would be \[ \frac{27+18+12+9+3}{5} = 13.8 \text{ minutes}\] The bus is three times faster than walking. Because there is only one bus stop, students will need to walk either to the bus stop or to school.
    1. Determine the average travel time from each location when the bus stop is placed at a. Repeat this for placing the stop at b through e.
    2. Which location is the optimal bus stop to minimize the average travel time?
    3. How big would the weight on the a—b edge need to become to change the location that minimizes the average travel time?
